Driving in high heels is dangerous!
There are many accidents where young people step on the wrong pedal (Auto Messe Web) - Yahoo! News
Violation of the Road Traffic Act
Reports of accidents caused by mishandling, such as stepping on the wrong pedal, are increasing and attracting attention, but it is a big mistake to think that this is a problem only for elderly drivers.
According to the 2015 National Police Agency data, the most common age at which pedal stepping accidents occurred was 1080 for those aged 24 and under, exceeding the 1,032 for those aged 75 and over.
Stepping on the wrong foot is not common among the elderly, and it is important to caution the younger generation as well.
Do you want to look stylish on your feet?
You want to make sure we're safe?
Although there is no description of the gender ratio or cause in the data, compared to older adults, who are more likely to be due to physical decline such as driving skills or reflexes, younger adults may make careless mistakes when they are distracted by other things such as music or conversation, or may make mistakes in stepping due to clothing or other factors.
Some women, in particular, opt for high heels, pumps, sandals and mules, as well as a variety of shoes depending on their outfit of the day, before driving off.
Some people find it troublesome to change their shoes, knowing they are not suited to driving, but many are driving without any ill will, with no thought to the possibility of causing danger.
However, driving in high heels and sandals is against the Road Traffic Act.
The first is Article 70, "Violation of safe driving duty" which stipulates "Ensuring control of the steering wheel, brake or other operations" and "Driving in a manner that may cause an accident and cause harm to people".
The other is Article 71, which stipulates that the driver must comply with the matters set forth by the respective prefectural public safety committees. For example, the Miyazaki Prefecture Road Traffic Law Enforcement bylaw stipulates in Article 12 that "the driver must not drive a vehicle (excluding a light vehicle) wearing geta, slippers, high heels or other footwear that may interfere with the driver's operation, or wearing footwear in a manner that interferes with the driver's operation."
Each prefecture has the same intent to consider driving with "footwear that may interfere with driving operation" as a violation, even if it is not specified as high heels.
So why is it dangerous to drive in high heels?
That's obvious given the basics of safe pedal operation.
For safe and reliable pedal operation, first secure the heel of your foot to the floor, place your foot so that it is perpendicular to the brake pedal, and when you step on the accelerator, use that as a fulcrum and lean your toe to the right.
Regardless of the behavior of the car, it is important to keep the heel firmly anchored so that it does not shake.
However, with high heels the area where the heel is grounded to the floor is small, and it is always wobbly, and because the heel is high and the whole foot is angled, the area of the toe that is grounded to the pedal is also small, so it is difficult to step on firmly, and it is difficult to adjust finely.
This is believed to be a risk.
Most cars have floor carpets, but this material can easily catch the heel of a high heel, preventing the pedal from stepping on it when it suddenly breaks, or when the heel gets caught and the high heel takes off, causing it to get stuck in the back of the pedal and become impossible to step on.
There was also a case where the heel got stuck on the back of the pedal when stepping on it and then returning it, preventing the accelerator from returning.

One year since the East Iburi earthquake: Securing water even in the event of a power outage A condominium in Hakodate that is installing "sprinkler plugs" directly connected to water pipes: Hokkaido Shimbun Newspaper Doshin Online Edition
Measures are being implemented at condominiums in Hakodate city, where water was cut off in response to a total power outage (blackout) caused by the eastern Iburi earthquake last September.
According to the Department of Water and Sewerage in the city's Bureau of Business, seven condominiums have installed "watering plugs" in the year since the earthquake, which can discharge water in the event of a power outage.
There are about 400 four-story or more condominiums and private communal housing units in the city that are "receiving water" that may be cut off in the event of a power outage, and the department is calling for action to be considered.
There are two main types of water supply for houses: The "receiving system," in which water is temporarily stored in a receiving tank located underground or on the roof, and the "direct connection system," in which water is supplied directly from the city's water distribution pipes to each household.
Even if the water pipe is not damaged, if the electric pump that pumps water from the receiving tank to each household stops working due to a power outage, etc., the water supply will not be possible and each household will be cut off.

Cutting-edge art that invites you to another world Okayama art exchange opens in the city center: Sanyo Newspaper Digital | San-digi
People walking around the classroom that was the setting for a video work depicting the future 3,000 years from now, at the former Uchiyamashita Elementary School
The international art exhibition "Okayama Art Exchange 2019" (organized by the executive committee of the city, prefecture, etc.), held every three years in the center of Okayama City, opened on the 27th.
Art fans in Japan and overseas are enjoying the cutting-edge art created by 18 artists from 9 countries, in line with the mysterious title "IF THE SNAKE: The Snake".
For this second edition, following the one in 2016, the French artist Pierre Huig has been appointed as the artistic director, and he has developed works that make use of the climate and local resources of Okayama.
Thirteen locations including the former Uchishitayama Elementary School (Marunouchi, Kita-ku, Okayama City) and the Prefectural Tenjinyama Cultural Plaza (Tenjinmachi, Okayama City) will showcase about 40 works, including video and sculptures.
An opening ceremony was held at the same elementary school before the opening ceremony, which was attended by about 100 people involved.
Chairman of the executive committee, Mayor Omori Masao, and general producer Ishikawa Yasuharu gave speeches, saying, "We have a collection of works that stimulate intellectual sensitivity, imagination, and creativity. I hope this exhibition will attract the world's attention."
Families and out-of-school children immediately visited the school grounds, where they saw a snake-shaped robot that wiggled in response to the sounds around it, and toured the school grounds, which had been transformed into a stage for a video work depicting the world 3,000 years from now.
I was enjoying the art that took me to another world.
One fifteenth-year high school student in Tokyo, who is traveling with his father, said, "It's interesting to have a work that makes use of the atmosphere of the old school building. It makes me imagine the time that passed in this place."
Closed on Mondays (or the following day if it is a national holiday) until November 24.
Tens of thousands of people rally in Hong Kong in 5th year of Umbrella Movement, aiming for universal suffrage | Shizuoka &lt;unk&gt; S
People gathered at the rally site on the 28th, marking the 5th anniversary of the Umbrella Movement (pool photo)
Hong Kong: Tens of thousands of people gathered in a park close to the Hong Kong government headquarters building in Admiralty, where the protesters' biggest stronghold was, on the 28th, five years after the Umbrella Movement, a massive demonstration in 2014 calling for democracy in the election of the Hong Kong chief executive.
Around the venue, some young people occupied a major road and threw Molotov cocktails at the government building.
As the protests triggered by the proposed amendment to the Fugitive Offenders Act continue, participants have renewed their determination to fight for "true universal suffrage," which they were unable to win at the time.
The protests, which began in earnest in June, have now grown to 112 days, more than the 79 days of the Umbrella Movement, and the protesters' demands have expanded to include "five demands," such as electoral reform.
Ibaraki National Sports Festival begins today 28th | Sports | Saga Shimbun News | Saga Shimbun live
The 74th National Sports Festival "Ikiiki Ibaraki Yume National Sports Festival 2019" will hold the general opening ceremony on the 28th at Kasamatsu Sports Park Athletics Stadium in Hitachinaka City, Ibaraki Prefecture, and the heated game will start for 11 days until October 8.
About 400 players and coaches from Saga Prefecture will participate in 33 sports (excluding those held before the exhibition), and they will take on strong teams from all over the country with pride in their homeland.
The prefecture had been languishing in 43rd place for three years in a row until two years ago, but rose to 33rd place last year.
They are also strengthening their preparations for the national sports tournament to be held in the prefecture in 2023, and this year they are aiming to be in the top 20.
In the sports before the exhibition, which was held until the 16th, Saga high school boys' and girls' rhythmic gymnastics, as well as adult girls and boys' and girls' gymnastics, all gained momentum by finishing in sixth place.
I want to follow the flow of each game and try to make it to the top.
